Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug (1914-2009) was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the extensive increases in agricultural production that were termed the Green Revolution.

Borlaug received his Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota in 1942. He then worked as a microbiologist for DuPont until 1944, when he joined the Rockefeller Foundation's Cooperative Mexican Agricultural Program. In Mexico, he worked to develop high-yielding strains of wheat that were resistant to disease and environmental stress. His success in Mexico spurred the development of similar programs in other countries.

In 1970, Borlaug was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work in combating world hunger, and he continued his efforts until his death at age 95.
